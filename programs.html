Visual Arts

In Art, students explore a variety of materials and techniques while developing artistic behaviors — skills like observation, persistence, reflection, and creative risk-taking that transfer across all areas of learning.

Anchor Standards:

  • Create — artwork using a variety of materials and techniques
  • Perceive — select, analyze, and interpret artistic work for presentation
  • Respond — interpret intent and meaning in artistic work
  • Connect — relate artistic ideas to societal, cultural, and historical contexts

Artsonia: Our digital portfolio platform. Look for an email from Artsonia and click to give permission — you can follow your artist's progress across grades 6, 7 & 8.

Required Supplies: 9×12" mixed media sketchbook (wire spiral, 30+ pages), #2 pencils with erasers, headphones/earbuds, and a UA binder with folders.

Artistic Behaviors

  • Develop Craft
  • Observe & Envision
  • Engage & Persist
  • Reflect
  • Stretch & Explore
  • Express & Connect

Why Art Matters

  • Nearly 90% believe arts should be in K–12
  • Benefits students across all subject areas
  • Teaches skills employers are looking for
  • Supports mental health and well-being

Band

Band is a year-round Unified Arts class with five primary components: group lessons, RAMS block rehearsal, after-school rehearsal, at-home practice, and concerts.

What to expect:

  • Group lessons once per week during school
  • RAMS block rehearsal once per week
  • After-school rehearsal: Thursdays 2:30–3:30 (Grade 6); late bus at 4:00
  • Practice at home: 10–20 minutes/day, 3–4 times per week
  • Two yearly concerts at HHS (December & May)

Learning Objectives

  • Improve individual technique and tone
  • Rehearse and prepare music for performances
  • Work collaboratively as an ensemble
  • Improve music literacy and note reading
  • Develop musical improvisation skills

Jazz Lab (Grade 6)

  • Explores jazz articulation & improvisation
  • Performs in Winter and Spring concerts
  • Meets Thursdays 3:30–4:30 after Band
  • Open to all students in Band

Chorus

Chorus meets twice per week — once during class time and once during RAMS block, where all chorus classes combine to rehearse together.

Goals:

  • Become better musicians and singers
  • Work together as a group
  • Better sight-read rhythms and pitches
  • Learn music from a variety of genres and cultures
  • Appreciate singing as part of something larger

Students are expected to practice music at home when practice assignments are posted on Google Classroom.

Schedule

  • Class time: once per week
  • RAMS Block: combined rehearsal
  • Two concerts per year at HHS

Select Choir

  • Leadership group for top singers
  • Learn to lead rehearsals and conduct
  • Advanced harmonization skills
  • After school 2:30–3:30
  • Entry by audition

General Music

General Music provides students with many different ways to engage with music on their own, outside of the classroom. Learning is focused into four main categories aligned with the National Standards.

National Standards Focus Areas:

  • Creating — composing and arranging music
  • Performing — playing and singing
  • Connecting — relating music to the world
  • Responding — analyzing and evaluating music

Units

  • Piano
  • Guitar
  • Music Technology (GarageBand, Soundtrap)
  • Music Analysis
  • Composition 1 — Melody Writing
  • Composition 2 — Putting It All Together

Computer Science & Digital Literacy

Over three years, students build essential digital skills, practice critical thinking, and develop responsible technology habits. Each year introduces new and exciting topics, progressing from foundational skills to advanced coding and emerging technologies.

Assignments are posted in Google Classroom. Students who need extra time can complete work during RAMS, lunch, or after school.

Grade 6

  • Digital Skills for Middle School
  • Karel Adventures (Intro to Coding)
  • Exploring Digital Citizenship
  • Exploring the Web & Web Design
  • Virtual Reality

Grade 7

  • Digital Problem Solving
  • Code with Karel
  • Art with Code
  • Creating Apps with MIT App Inventor
  • Game Design & Computing

Grade 8

  • Python with Karel
  • Game Development with p5.play
  • AI for Middle School
  • Cybersecurity and You!

Health

Students have Health once per week. They learn skills needed to make healthy choices both in and out of school. Encourage your child to participate in class discussions by raising their hand and answering questions.

Students should bring:

  • Something to write with
  • Fully charged Chromebook
  • Unified Arts binder

Grade 6

  • Accessing valid health info & resources
  • Enhancing personal safety
  • Goal setting for healthy habits
  • Gender identity & sexual orientation
  • Healthy vs. unhealthy relationships

Grade 7

  • Substance use and misuse
  • Managing stress & mental health
  • Nutrition and health risks
  • Digital safety
  • Sexual readiness & boundaries

Grade 8

  • Analyzing influences on health
  • Decision making & refusal skills
  • Effective communication in relationships
  • Responsible decisions about sexual health
  • Consent, coercion & exploitation

Physical Education

Students have PE once per week for 42 minutes. We use a 3-year rotation for our PE curriculum, so students in all grades learn the same topics each year.

Come prepared:

  • Wear comfortable, flexible clothing for ALL movement
  • Wear sneakers (no Crocs, sandals, boots, etc.)
  • Bring a water bottle

If your child is injured: send a written note or email. A doctor's note is required if they'll miss 2+ classes. List restrictions and approved activities if possible.

Year C (2025–2026)

  • Unit 1: Cooperative Games
  • Unit 2: Disc Golf
  • Unit 3: Tchoukball
  • Unit 4: Functional Fitness / Weight Room
  • Unit 5: Pickleball
  • Unit 6: Group Dance Rope
  • Unit 7: Backyard Games

Wellness 2

Wellness 2 is a non-traditional wellness class based on the Wellness standards taught in grades 6–8. It focuses on more targeted areas of support around students' social, emotional, and physical well-being, with small class sizes of about 7–12 students.

Curriculum

  • Nature Walks
  • Mindfulness & Meditation
  • Yoga
  • Executive Functioning Skills
  • Mental Health Strategies
  • Social Pragmatics
  • Stress/Anxiety Prevention
  • Self Management
